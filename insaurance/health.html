Understanding Health Insurance

Health insurance is a financial safety net that covers medical expenses such as hospitalisation, doctor consultations, surgeries, medicines, and diagnostic tests arising due to illness or injury.

In today’s fast-paced lifestyle, medical emergencies can occur without warning and often come with high treatment costs. A comprehensive health insurance plan ensures access to quality healthcare while protecting your savings from unexpected financial strain.

Choosing the right health insurance not only helps manage healthcare expenses but also offers peace of mind for you and your family by providing timely support during critical medical situations.

Key Coverage Areas

Hospitalisation

Covers expenses related to inpatient treatment and surgeries.

Medical Treatments

Includes diagnostic tests, procedures, and prescribed care.

Critical Illness Support

Provides financial assistance during serious medical conditions.

Preventive Care

Supports regular health check-ups and early detection.

Types of Health Insurance Plans

  • Individual Health Insurance – Coverage for one person
  • Family Floater Plans – Shared coverage for family members
  • Senior Citizen Plans – Designed for elderly healthcare needs
  • Critical Illness Plans – Lump sum support for major illnesses
  • Group Health Insurance – Employee and corporate coverage

Frequently Asked Questions

How much health insurance coverage do I need? +

Coverage depends on your age, medical history, family size, lifestyle, and expected healthcare expenses. Higher coverage ensures better financial protection.

Is a family floater plan better than individual plans? +

Family floater plans are cost-effective when healthcare needs are moderate and shared, while individual plans offer separate coverage limits.

Does health insurance cover pre-existing diseases? +

Yes, most policies cover pre-existing conditions after a specified waiting period, which varies by insurer and plan.
